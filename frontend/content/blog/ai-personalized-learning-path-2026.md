## What Is a Personalized Learning Path?

A personalized learning path is a sequence of topics, exercises, and resources that adapts specifically to *you*. Instead of marching through a fixed curriculum, the system adjusts based on:

- What you already know (diagnostic assessment)
- Where you're struggling (real-time performance tracking)
- How you learn best (pacing, format, depth)
- What your end goal is (exam prep, skill building, curiosity)

The result is a roadmap that skips what you've mastered, slows down on weak spots, and routes you toward your goal as efficiently as possible.

## How AI Builds Your Learning Path

### Step 1: Diagnostic Assessment

Before anything else, a good AI system figures out where you stand. This might be a short quiz, a few open-ended questions, or analysis of your past performance on similar material.

Shallow diagnostics ask "what topics do you want to learn?" Deep diagnostics find out what you actually understand — and there's a big difference.

### Step 2: Knowledge Graph Mapping

AI systems map out the subject as a network of concepts. To understand calculus, you need limits. To understand limits, you need algebra. The AI finds your current position in that graph and plans the shortest path to your target.

### Step 3: Adaptive Sequencing

As you work through material, the AI tracks what you get right, what you skip, how long you spend, and where you lose confidence. It uses this signal to re-sequence upcoming content in real time.

This is very different from "pick your own pace." The AI is actively steering, not just unlocking modules.

### Step 4: Spaced Repetition Integration

Concepts you've learned don't vanish from your path — they get scheduled for review at intervals that maximize retention. This is spaced repetition: revisiting material just before you'd forget it.

### Step 5: Continuous Re-evaluation

Good personalized learning never locks in a fixed path. Every session updates the model. If you suddenly grasp a concept that was giving you trouble, the system adjusts. If a new gap appears, it routes around it.

## What Makes a Personalized Learning Path Actually Work

Not all "personalized" systems are equal. Here's what separates genuinely adaptive platforms from ones that just let you choose a difficulty setting:

**Real diagnostics vs. self-reporting.** If the system just asks what you want to learn, it's not adapting — it's customizing. True adaptation measures actual understanding, not stated preference.

**Micro-adjustments, not just module unlocking.** Personalization should happen at the question level, not just "complete unit 1 to unlock unit 2."

**Conversation, not just content.** When you're confused, you need to ask a question and get a specific answer. Static content — however well-sequenced — can't do that.

**Honest feedback.** The best adaptive systems tell you when you're ready to move on and when you're not, even if you'd prefer to skip ahead.

## Building Your Own Adaptive Study System

If your platform doesn't offer full personalization, you can approximate it:

1. **Start with a diagnostic.** Quiz yourself on prerequisites before starting a new topic. Note what you miss.
2. **Map concepts before studying.** Write out what you think you need to learn and what connects to what.
3. **Use spaced repetition for review.** Anki is free and highly effective for this.
4. **Talk through concepts.** Explaining something out loud (or to an AI classmate) reveals gaps faster than re-reading.
5. **Track your weak spots.** Keep a running list of concepts you got wrong and revisit them regularly.
