The transition from high school to college is one of the most stressful times in a student's life. However, in 2026, the college admissions process is more streamlined than ever, thanks to a robust ecosystem of digital tools and AI assistants.

## The Essentials: Common App and College Board

- **Common App**: The primary platform for applying to over 1,000 colleges. In 2026, the **Common App** mobile experience allows you to track your deadlines, request recommendations, and even draft your essay on the go.
- **College Board**: The home of the SAT and AP exams. Their app provides personalized practice based on your previous scores and helps you find scholarships through "BigFuture."

## Managing Your Schedule: Coursicle and Quest

- **Coursicle**: A must-have for planning your future college schedule. It helps you design your perfect week and alerts you when a seat opens up in a "Full" class.
- **Quest**: Often used for university-specific portal access, **Quest** (or similar systems like **Navigate 360**) helps students stay on top of their orientation tasks.

## Why AI is a Game-Changer

AI advisors are now integrated into many of these platforms. They can suggest colleges based on your **GPA** in **PowerSchool** or your **vocabulary** level in **language learning** apps. Tools like **Study Fetch** can even help you turn your SAT prep into interactive quizzes.

## Pro-Tips for Applicants:
- Use **Quest** for early-action tracking.
- Link your **College Board** account to Khan Academy for free, tailored SAT prep.
- Check **Coursicle** daily during registration week.
