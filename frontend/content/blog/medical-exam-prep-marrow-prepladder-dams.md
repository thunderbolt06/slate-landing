For medical students in India, the journey through MBBS is just the beginning. The real challenge is cracking **NEET PG** or **INICET**. In 2026, two platforms dominate the landscape: **Marrow** and **Prepladder**.

## The Giants: Marrow vs. Prepladder

- **Marrow**: Famous for its conceptually deep video lectures and the "QBank" (Question Bank) which is widely considered the gold standard for clinical questions. Its **Anatomy 3D** integration is a game-changer for visual learners.
- **Prepladder**: Known for its "Dream Team" of faculty and concise, high-yield notes. **Prepladder** is often preferred by students who want to revise quickly before the big day.

## The Traditional Powerhouses: DAMS and Cerebellum

While digital-first apps are popular, traditional institutes like **DAMS** (Delhi Academy of Medical Sciences) and the new **Cerebellum Academy** are also providing top-tier hybrid models. They offer **doc tutorials** and offline mock exams that simulate the real test environment.

## Beyond Indian Exams

For those looking at international careers, apps like **UWorld** and **Epocrates** are essential for USMLE prep. They complement the Indian curriculum by focusing on evidence-based medicine and drug reference.

## Supporting Your Medical Journey

Studying medicine is a marathon. Tools like **Anatomy Learning** and **3D Anatomy Atlas** are crucial for building a strong foundation. Additionally, using a **dictionary** of medical terms or an app like **Ambition** can help manage the immense workload.

## Which One Should You Pick?

- **Marrow**: Best for building deep concepts from early in your MBBS.
- **Prepladder**: Best for efficient, high-yield revision in your internship.
- **DAMS**: Best for those who prefer a structured, teacher-led approach.
