With the rise of remote learning and online assessments, two major concerns have emerged: academic integrity and student privacy. In 2026, schools are using sophisticated tools to balance the need for secure testing with the protection of student data.

## Ensuring Integrity: Respondus Lockdown Browser

**Respondus Lockdown Browser** is the industry standard for secure exams. When a student uses it, they are unable to print, copy, go to another URL, or access other applications. In 2026, it often integrates with AI proctoring that can detect irregular behavior without needing a human to watch the entire session.

## Protecting Students: Clever and Raptor Alert

Safe browsing is more than just blocking websites; it's about managing identity.
- **Clever**: Provides a secure Single Sign-On (SSO) for students. Instead of remembering 20 passwords, students log in once through **Clever**, and the school ensures that only approved apps (like **Google Classroom** or **Canvas**) can access their data.
- **Raptor Alert**: A safety management system used by schools to manage visitors and emergencies, ensuring that the physical and digital campus remains secure.

## The Privacy Balance

While tools like **Respondus Lockdown Browser** are necessary for high-stakes testing, students must also be aware of their "digital footprint." Using a **parent app** to manage privacy settings and being mindful of which **kids games** or **math ai** tools have access to your camera and microphone is essential in 2026.

## Safety First Checklist:
- Use **Clever** for all school logins.
- Check permissions on **homework helper** apps.
- Understand the "Privacy Policy" of your **dictionary** or **translator** tool.
