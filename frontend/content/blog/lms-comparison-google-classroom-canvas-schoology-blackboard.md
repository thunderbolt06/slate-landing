Choosing an **LMS** is rarely a consumer choice—students inherit **Google Classroom**, **Canvas**, **Schoology**, **Blackboard**, or **Moodle** from their district or university. Search demand still spikes for **canvas student**, **google classroom**, **classroom**, **schoology**, **blackboard**, and **moodle** because those names are how people find login paths, mobile apps, and integrations.

## What each system tends to optimize for

**Google Classroom** — Fast assignment loops and Google file sharing; huge in both **United States** and **India** app charts.  
**Canvas** — Rubrics, outcomes, and a mature mobile experience (**Canvas Student**).  
**Schoology** — K–12 districts and parent comms; often next to **PowerSchool** / SIS layers.  
**Blackboard** — Legacy higher-ed footprints; migration stories are common.  
**Moodle** — Open-source control; popular globally when institutions self-host.

## Where AI fits without breaking trust

Good AI hygiene: generate **explainers** and **practice variants** outside the locked assessment window. Tools like **NotebookLM** love exported PDFs; **Slate Up** can turn a study guide prompt into a narrated walkthrough. Pair either with **Quizlet** / **Anki** for retrieval.
